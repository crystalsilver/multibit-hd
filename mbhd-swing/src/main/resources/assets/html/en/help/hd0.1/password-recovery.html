Restore password

If you forget your wallet password, then you need to restore your password from seed.

Step 1 - Locate your seed phrase

To restore your wallet and recover your digibyte, you must have your seed phrase.

With your seed phrase, you can recover your password, and so recover your digibyte. You must keep your seed phrase safe, because anyone who knows your seed phrase can restore your wallet, and steal your digibyte.

Step 2 - From the Unlock screen select "Restore" then select "Restore password from seed"

Follow the instructions on the DigiBit HD screen.

If you have also lost your seed phrase, you cannot restore the password

If you're certain you typed in your password correctly, but DigiBit is not unlocking your wallet, you can:

  1. Make sure that you are using the most recent password you know.
  2. Try combinations - change one character at a time to upper- or lowercase, swap characters, add a ! to the end and so on
  3. If the password uses non-English characters consider the effect of the local language miscoding them

As a last resort go to a quiet room and relax. Think about where you might have put a copy of the password. Perhaps one of the following:

  • Hidden file somewhere?
  • USB drive?
  • Encrypted copy held on LastPass or KeePass? You should be using a secure password generator with 14 or more random characters.
  • Accidental copy on a local backup service like Time Machine or rsync?
  • An intentional encrypted copy on Dropbox or GDrive?
  • Maybe you wrote it down and put it in a book, or an old drawer?
  • Does a trusted friend or relative have a copy?

If you have really, truly lost or forgotten both your password and your seed phrase, then it is likely that you have lost access to your digibyte. There is absolutely no way that the DigiBit team can possibly recover them.

You should keep the encrypted wallet files safe just in case inspiration strikes and you somehow remember the password.
